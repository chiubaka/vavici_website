Regina Benjamin

Born on October 26, 1956, in Mobile, Alabama, Regina Benjamin became a clinician who served the fishing community of Bayou La Batre, providing much needed medical care. She has been recognized widely for her humanitarian spirit and was appointed the 18th U.S. surgeon general by President Barack Obama, with Benjamin pushing forth wellness and prevention initiatives. She resigned in 2013.She went to Xavier University of Louisiana, graduating with a B.S. in 1979—and also interning with the C.I.A.—before attending Morehouse School of Medicine and receiving an M.D. from the University of Alabama, Birmingham, in 1984.
